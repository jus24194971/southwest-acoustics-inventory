The Great Listing Round-Up

aka “Reconcile” — your computer now does your least favorite chore.

Hey Dad. You've got the same guitars and parts scattered across Squarespace, Reverb, and eBay like a yard sale that got out of hand. Matching them all up by hand sounds like a special kind of punishment — so I built a robot for it. It's called Reconcile, it lives under Listings → Reconcile, and here's how to boss it around.

1

Round 'em up — the scrape

  • Paste your eBay store link in the box, hit Save.
  • Click Scrape all my listings. Sit back ~15 seconds while it hauls in everything you have on all three sites. Yes, even that one.
2

Let it match them up — the AI bit

  • Click Match with AI. It introduces your “1962 Strat” on eBay to your “Vintage Strat (Sunburst!!)” on Reverb so they finally realize they're the same guitar.
  • Skim the cards. If it ever pairs two different things, smack detach on the impostor and tell it why — it learns from that (unlike some people).
  • Tap Validate match on the ones that look right.
It can tell a Tele from a Strat — partly because it looks at the photo, and partly because I threatened it. If it still slips up, that's what “detach” is for.
3

Walk the line — the Review Wizard

Hit Review wizard. It shows you one thing at a time so you never feel buried. Coffee in hand, just react:

  • New finds it scraped → Have it (punch in how many you've got — it fills in the brand, model, color, even reads the photo; nod and Save), Future (you'll carry it, not yet), or No longer have.
  • Stuff already in your inventory → Keep, Never carry again (retires it — but keeps a full copy you can un-retire later), or Skip.
When you hit “Have it,” the listing photos ride along on the form so you can eyeball the item while you check the details. Your job is mostly to nod and click Save. It's a good job.
4

Take out the trash — Dead Listings

  • Everything you flagged “no longer have” piles up on the Dead listings page (Listings menu).
  • Hit Remove (or Remove all) and it yanks them off Squarespace and Reverb for you.
  • For eBay, click open, end it on eBay yourself, then tap Mark ended. (eBay won't let outside apps end its old listings, because eBay is eBay.)
Nothing comes down by accident. A live listing only disappears when you personally click Remove on this page. Until then, mash buttons fearlessly — you cannot break a thing.
